What Are Cookies

Cookies are small pieces of text sent to your web browser by a website you visit. A cookie file is stored in your web browser and allows the Service or a third-party to recognise you and make your next visit easier and the Service more useful to you.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your personal computer or mobile device when you go offline, while session cookies are deleted as soon as you close your web browser.

For more general information on cookies and how to disable them, please consult the information at http://www.allaboutcookies.org.

How We Use Cookies

We use both session and persistent cookies for the following purposes:

  • To personalise our Service, such as remembering a user's information or preferences.
  • To authenticate users and prevent fraudulent or unauthorised use of user accounts.
  • To collect aggregate site usage metrics such as total number of visitors and pages viewed.
  • To monitor and operate third-party advertising campaigns.
  • To operate referral and/or affiliate programmes.

If you delete or refuse cookies on our website, you may be unable to access certain areas of our website and certain features may not work as expected.
