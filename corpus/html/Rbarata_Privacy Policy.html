If you select “Remember Me,” your login will persist for two weeks. If you log out of your account, the login cookies will be removed.

If you edit or publish an article, an additional cookie will be saved in your browser. This cookie includes no personal data and simply indicates the post ID of the article you just edited. It expires after 1 day.

If you accept our cookies policy when you visit our website, that information will expire in one year.

Where we send your data

Visitor comments are checked through an automated spam detection service (Akismet Anti-Spam).

Akismet collects information about visitors who comment. The information it collects includes the commenter’s IP address, user agent, referrer, and site URL (along with other information directly provided by the commenter such as their name, username, email address, and the comment itself).

Visitors are checked through an automated firewall (WordFence), which will collect your IP, location, and internet operator. However, we only check this information if WordFence sends a spam alert or website breach-related to the IP, which is rare since the IP blocking in the circumstances above is an automated feature.

We also use reCaptcha. ReCAPTCHA helps websites and mobile apps prevent spam and block “bots.” The program determines whether the user is a real person or a “bot” to stop “automated software” from weighing down your site.

ReCAPTCHA works by implementing an algorithm to analyze a user’s activity by taking a user’s browser window screenshot. If the activity is deemed as mechanical or a made-up word is used, the activity is flagged by the system.

Activity and user information reCAPTCHA collects and analyzes the following:

  • Typing patterns of the user
  • The amount of mouse clicks a user has done on the site or touches on an app
  • What language the user’s browser is using
  • Google cookies that have been placed on the site
  • The answers to question fields on the site
  • CSS information
  • Plug-ins installed on the browser

The algorithm also recognizes IP addresses that have been previously recognized as humans through cookies.

We use the version V3. ReCAPTCHA v3 doesn’t rely on users clicking checkboxes or pictures. The version 3 collects and analyzes the interactions a user has with a site to create a score. The final score indicates whether the activity is suspicious. This version decreases “user friction” by doing away with questions and creating a seamless user experience.

Registered users are also checked through the services above.
