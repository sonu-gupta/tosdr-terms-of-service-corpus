We at TaxAct, Inc. ("TaxAct") respect your privacy and the confidentiality of your personal information. This Privacy Statement explains what information TaxAct collects, how we use and disclose that information, the measures we take to keep it safe, and how users can exercise their rights with respect to their information. This Privacy Statement applies to all TaxAct websites, products, services, and applications that link to it. it also applies to in-person, over-the-phone, electronic communications and online services provided by TaxAct (collectively, the "Services").

We may change this Privacy Statement from time to time. If we make changes, we will notify you by revising the date at the top of the statement and, in some cases, we may provide you with additional notice (such as sending you an email notification or posting a banner notice on our web page). We encourage you to review the Privacy Statement whenever you access the Services or otherwise interact with us to stay informed about our information practices. If you have an unresolved privacy or data use concern that you feel we have not addressed satisfactorily, please contact our U.S. based third party dispute resolution provider (free of charge) at https://feedback-form.truste.com/watchdog/request.

Information Collected

Our primary goals in collecting information are to provide and improve our websites, Services, features and content, to administer your use of our site, and to enable our customers to enjoy and easily navigate our website and Services.

We collect personal information that you provide when you register for and use the Services:

  • In order to use or download our tax software, you are required to complete the registration form that includes contact information (such as name, email address, and phone number).

  • If you decide to prepare or file a tax return using the Services, we collect all the information you provide, including your Name, Social Security Number, Driver’s License number, and information about your employment, income, deductions, and any other information included in your return. We refer to this information as "Tax Return Information."

  • If you upload a form or other file from your desktop or mobile device, we collect and store those files and/or data.

  • If you decide to purchase our Services, we may request additional information, including payment information. If you pay using credit card, we or our third-party payment processor may collect financial information such as credit card number and expiration date. As an added layer of protection, credit card information is encrypted via a tokenization method.

  • When you use our customer service functions or consult with our personnel providing expertise related to our Service offerings, or otherwise contact or request contact from our service personnel, we may collect information about the questions or concerns you submit. In order to improve the quality of TaxAct's Services, TaxAct records service calls (after informing you during that call and before the recording starts) or support chat sessions. We may also ask to share your screen for the purpose of resolving your inquiry. When we do so, these screen share sessions may also be recorded for the same purposes. Finally, some interactions with our service staff may be via video conferencing and, in such circumstance, the video may be recorded in addition to the audio of the interaction.

We may also collect:

  • Information provided as part of customer testimonials, surveys, promotions, contests, sweepstakes, marketing events, trade shows, webinars, conferences, customer research, feedback or a similar event or occurrence where you provide personal information.

  • Information from your use of our referral service to tell a friend about our site, we will ask you for your friend's name and email address.

  • Information from publicly available sources, affiliated companies, or third parties, such as when we partner with other companies or use service providers to help us optimize our marketing activities.

Certain personal information is required to purchase, download, and effectively use our Services. If you ask us to delete such information or do not consent to our collection and use of this information, this may result in a termination of your user account and/or your ability to use the Services. If you are a resident of California and you are seeking to exercise rights under the California Consumer Protection Act, please see the section below titled “Your California Privacy Rights.”

Automatically Collected Information (Cookies, Browser Information, IP addresses, etc.)

TaxAct collects information about your browser or device as it interacts with the Services. This information includes your IP address and device identification information. We log information about your use of the Services, including the type of browser you use, access times, pages viewed, your IP address and the page you visited before navigating to our Services. We may keep track of how you interact with links on the Services, including emails, third-party services, and client applications. We do this to help analyze and improve the Services. Link information is usually only analyzed on an aggregated basis.

We also collect information about the computer or mobile device you use to access our Services, including the hardware model, operating system version, mobile network information, information indicating the device's physical location, and other information to allow us to identify the computer or device you use. We may combine this automatically collected information with other information we collect about you. We do this to improve services we offer you, and to improve marketing, analytics, and site functionality. If we send you a communication, such as an electronic mail, we may collect available information regarding your receipt and review of such communication.

Cookies are small data files stored on your hard drive or in device memory that help us improve our Services and your experience, enhance security, see which areas and features of our Services are popular and count visits. Web beacons are electronic images that may be used in our Services or emails and help deliver cookies, count visits and understand usage of our Services. You may elect to have your browser not accept cookies, however, this may disable or render unusable some of the features of the Services.

The use and disclosure of Tax Return Information is governed by Section 301-7216 of the Internal Revenue Code and many state codes and regulations. We use your Tax Return Information only in accordance with applicable laws, such as to prepare, assist in preparing, obtain, provide, or market services in connection with preparing your tax return. to allow tax professionals to assist you with questions or tax preparation. to provide you with the products and services you specifically request or consent to and other uses or disclosures as you expressly consent to from time to time. or as required by law.

We use registration information and other information you provide, including personal information, to complete orders, for the purposes listed above, to research and address any questions or problems submitted through our technical support and customer service systems, to send you product or technical notices, updates, and security alerts, as well as to notify you if there are issues with your order or when new/enhanced products and services become available. We may use your information to analyze and improve our service offerings to you, provide a customized and/or personalized experience, and protect the safety and security of TaxAct, our Services, our customers and others. We may use your information to show you tailored advertising, to send you marketing messages, or if you choose to use our referral service to tell a friend about our site, we will send your friend an email inviting them to visit the site.

We also use the information we collect to monitor and investigate fraud, identity theft, and other illegal activity and to personalize and improve the Services.

TaxAct values the privacy and security of consumer information and unless (i) it is allowed by applicable law (including, where applicable, Section 301-7216 of the Internal Revenue Service’s code). and (ii) we have consent, we do not:

  • Sell or rent personal information we collect from our customers.

  • Use customer Tax Return Information to market products or services unrelated to tax preparation to consumers. or

  • Share consumer personal information we collect with third parties for use in their own business (i.e., unrelated to TaxAct providing products or services).

Regardless, you may elect to add your name to our “Do Not Sell” list by visiting https://www.taxact.com/do-not-sell-personal-information or by clicking the button labeled “Do Not Sell My Personal Information” on any TaxAct web page. We may share your information with our affiliated companies to assist in providing and maintaining the Services or, if we have received your consent, for their own purposes. If you use our professional products to prepare the taxes of others, we may share your information with our affiliate companies including Avantax Wealth Management, so that they may notify you of products and services that may be relevant to you.
