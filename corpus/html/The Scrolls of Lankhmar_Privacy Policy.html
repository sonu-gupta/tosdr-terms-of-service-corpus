The Plain English Version

If there are any discrepancies between the “Plain English Version” and the “Legalese Version”, the “Legalese Version” will take precedence. I will, however, do my best not to mislead you.

  • The Scrolls of Lankhmar and yours truly, the curator of the Scrolls, has no interest in collecting or selling or giving away your personal information. This site exists as a resource to everyone. It is a public service and a labor of love. You may create an account if you would like to contribute or subscribe to an email digest containing updates to the wiki. You may even contribute to discussions. Either way, your email address, and name or username will be saved on our server to provide that service. That is all we need it for.
  • The Scrolls of Lankhmar uses “cookies” in order for the software to provide you a seamless experience. That is all. If you decline the use of cookies, the software should still be able to function.
  • In the past, I have used “Google Analytics” and briefly installed “DISQUS”. I have since removed all third-party applications that I know to be capable of tracking your use (excepting Cloudflare, see below).
  • This site is powered by the open source, wiki engine, Dokuwiki.
  • This site is subject to an extreme volume of 'bot' traffic, and as such, in order to keep the server costs affordable, all traffic is routed through the services of cloudflare.com whose privacy policy is posted. Their policy for “End Users” is the only one that would impact visitors to this site.

The Website uses “cookies” to help personalize your online experience. A cookie is a text file that is placed on your hard disk by a web page server. Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your computer. Cookies are uniquely assigned to you, and can only be read by a web server in the domain that issued the cookie to you. We may use cookies to collect, store, and track information for statistical purposes to operate our Website and Services. You have the ability to accept or decline cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer.

Do Not Track signals

Some browsers incorporate a Do Not Track feature that signals to websites you visit that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Tracking is not the same as using or collecting information in connection with a website. For these purposes, tracking refers to collecting personally identifiable information from consumers who use or visit a website or online service as they move across different websites over time. Our Website does not track its visitors over time and across third party websites. However, some third party sites may keep track of your browsing activities when they serve you content, which enables them to tailor what they present to you.
