This cookie statement was last amended on July 20th 2019. What are cookies

We use cookies on this website. A cookie is a text file that is sent along with the pages of this website and is stored by your browser on the hard drive of your computer, tablet and/or mobile phone. The information stored therein can be returned to our servers or to the servers of the third parties concerned during a future visit.

Why ArvanCloud uses cookies Functional cookies

Some cookies ensure that certain parts of the website work properly and that your user preferences remain known. For example, cookies are used to display fonts correctly, to remember your session of use on the web server so that you can view the website, or to remember a search term that searches within the website or a selected filter.

Google Analytics

We use cookies from Google Analytics to gain insight into the way in which website visitors use our website. We have concluded a data processing agreement with Google in which agreements are laid down on the handling of the collected data. Google is not allowed to share the data obtained or use it for other Google services and we do not allow IP addresses to be anonymised. Google may also transfer information to third parties where required to do so by law, or where such third parties process the information on Google's behalf. More information about the data processing by Google Analytics can be found in the privacy statement of Google and in the privacy policy of Google Analytics.

Social Media-cookies

The Social Media cookies that are placed when visiting our website make it possible for visitors who use these social media to share (parts of) our web pages with these social media networks. Once a visitor has logged in to the social media networks, the Social Media cookies can be qualified as 'functional cookies'. More information about the data processing by Facebook, Google+, LinkedIn and Twitter can be found in the privacy declaration of these online services.
