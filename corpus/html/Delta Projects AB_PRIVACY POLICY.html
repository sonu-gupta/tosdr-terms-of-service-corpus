PRIVACY POLICY Introduction

Delta Projects AB (org nr: 556622-4936) is a company providing proprietary digital advertising technology (the “Services”, the “Delta Projects Services”) that automates advertising and campaign execution and optimizes advertising campaigns. To give advertisers tailored campaigns with rich performance, full transparency and relevant targeting and better experience, through relevant advertisements, for consumers.

Delta Projects takes the private nature of your information very seriously. We strive to be transparent about how we collect and process information, how we keep information secure and provide you with meaningful choices. This policy regarding our privacy practices (the “Privacy Policy“) describes how we treat the information we collect via our Services and when you visit and use deltaprojects.com (the “Site“). This Privacy Policy applies to any processing of personal data where Delta Projects acts as the Data Controller. Delta Projects may also process personal data in the role of processor to our clients. Where the clients acts as the controller, their respective privacy policy is applicable

We adhere to applicable industry guidelines and principles and are an active member of industry associations that collectively govern policies around online consumer privacy and industry standards in the context of interest-based advertising, including: the Interactive Advertising Bureau (IAB), EDAA and AdChoices. We will continue to review and update our Privacy Policy and procedures to ensure the safety and protection of all collected information and we believe that these principles help protect individual consumer privacy.

 

Delta Projects Services Processed data

In the course of delivering an ad to you via our Services, Delta Projects does not intentionally collect information that reveals your real-world identity, such as your name, address, phone number, or the name of your pets. Delta Projects collects data about your browser or device through the use of cookies and similar technologies, as explained below. The non-sensitive information Delta Projects collects includes common information found in every communication sent over the Internet, from which we infer such things as: browser type (e.g., Chrome or Safari). operating system (e.g., Mac OSX or Windows). Internet Protocol (IP) address, that may be considered personal information is anonymized. and mobile advertising identifier (anonymized IDFA or AAID), for mobile devices such as smartphones and tablets. Delta Projects’ clients may also use our services to collect data regarding your interactions with them, including your use of our clients’ sites and mobile apps, the sites and apps our clients advertise on, and other pages on the Internet. We may also capture additional data in order to calculate a unique order identifier, as described below.

We may also receive Precise Location Data (as defined by the Network Advertising Initiative) generated from your use of your mobile device, such as the latitude/longitude coordinates provided by a mobile app publisher or other media supply source in conjunction with the opportunity to purchase advertising inventory for specific geographic location targeting. When we process Precise Location Data (as defined by the NAI), the mobile app publisher or other media supply source is responsible for enabling you to provide or withdraw consent with respect to the collection and use of your Precise Location Data.

We will collect personal data such as your name and e-mail address if you sign up for our newsletter or marketing e-mails, or if you contact us to request a demonstration of our products/services or to otherwise send us questions.

Sensitive personal data

Delta Projects does not knowingly create data segments that are based upon what we consider to be sensitive information (for example, we don’t create any data segments to determine credit worthiness, for insurance underwriting or similar purposes, nor do we create segments of EU consumers based on personal data considered to be sensitive in the EU, e.g. information about physical or mental health or condition, sex life, race or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or similar beliefs).

How the data is being collected

We may assign a unique online identifier to your browser or mobile device when we serve an ad to it or we identify it on a client’s website or mobile app. The unique online identifier may be stored in a cookie in your browser or mobile device. It helps us understand your interests and deliver more relevant ads to you. The unique online identifier and other information collected via the Services can also help our advertising clients measure your activity on their sites and determine the effectiveness of advertisements delivered via the Services. This information can then be used by our advertising clients to better cater to your needs and deliver ads to you for the types of products and services in which you might be interested.

None of these technologies enable us to personally identify an individual as yourself. Instead, they enable the Services to determine within a reasonable level of confidence that a browser or device is the same as one with which the Services has previously interacted. For instance, the Services can determine that a browser or device to which we served an ad on Monday may be the same browser or device for which we observed a purchase on Tuesday.

The Purpose of Processing

We use the information we collect to create data segments, and these data segments are generally based upon your interactions with our clients or other third party media suppliers, such as the websites that you visit and the mobile applications that you use.

Furthermore, we run analytics, target ads, and measure the effectiveness of ads on behalf of Delta Projects’ clients. We also use it to improve our Services that we provide to our clients. For these purposes we may share information with our clients, partners and service providers.

Cookies and Other Ad Targeting Technologies

Like most other digital marketing services, Delta Projects uses “cookies”, mobile advertising identifiers, and other technologies to enhance users’ experiences on the web, to deliver more relevant ads, and for ad delivery and reporting via the Services.

We use these technologies to help recognize a browser or device so that we and our clients are able to deliver relevant advertising to you, measure the impact of that advertising and better understand and recognize digital media usage patterns.

The types of technologies used via the Services include:

  • Cookies – When browsing a website, small pieces of information is sent between the website and the browser – a cookie. This information is stored as a text file in the browser, and is sent to the web site when performing a page view. The web site can send updated cookies to the web browser when the page is being displayed. For more general information about cookies, please visit http://www.allaboutcookies.org/cookies/Delta Projects services may use cookies that are bound to the domain de17a.com. Cookies stored for this domain contain primarily an anonymised unique online identifier. This unique identifier allows Delta Projects services to identify different web browsers and these cookies do not collect PII (Personally identifiable information).If a user has opted out of Delta Projects services, a cookie will be stored in their browser, containing information that the user does not wish to participate in Delta Projects behavioral targeting. If the user clears his or her cookies, the user will have to opt-out of the services again.
  • Mobile Advertising Identifiers – Mobile advertising IDs (e.g., Apple’s IDFA or Google’s AAID) are unique IDs that are associated with individual mobile devices and that do not reveal your real world identity. They usually are user-resettable.
  • Tags – Tags are transparent images, iFrames, or JavaScript that our clients use to understand how browsers interact with them online, including on websites and in mobile apps.
  • Statistical Online Identifier – Statistical Online Identifiers are created via an algorithm using pseudonymous information about your browser or device, including your operating system, user-agent string, anonymized IP address, Internet browser and similar information. This information makes your browser or device distinct enough for our services to determine within a reasonable probability that they are encountering the same browser or device, including in environments where Delta Projects’ cookies are not supported.

These technologies are used to identify a browser or device within a certain level of confidence that enables the Services to function and serve more relevant ads.

Data Security and Protection

Delta Projects implement adequate security measures to protect all data collected and processed by Delta technology. At every step, Delta adapts relevant security and safety practices, and employs modern security hardware and software provided by recognised world-leading manufacturers.

Several data handling requirements are defined to appropriately safeguard information collected by Delta Projects. All data is stored on Delta Projects own hardware, or on cloud services, and Delta Projects has developed it’s own network structure for communication connected to the data. If data is being sent over a public network, such as the Internet, it is sent via an encrypted link.

Only high security personnel is allowed access to the data and our physical environment. Physical environment security is also ensured. Delta Projects solutions are hosted, and data is stored, in data centers with 4-layered security model with everything from perimeter fencing to customised security measures. The 4-layered physical access control includes:
• Perimeter fences, bollards, barriers
• Building-in-building construction
• 24x7x365 security guards, regular security patrols
• Centralised access control authorisation
• Man traps with weight measurement capability
• Role-based electronic badge access control
• Biometric access control
• Locked cabinets, cages, rooms
• Separate customer receiving area
• Separate packaging recovery area
• Video surveillance (internal and external)
