  18. GAMES OF SKILLA Game of Skill is a game where the outcome is determined mainly by mental or physical skill rather than by chance. Gamezop does not support, endorse or offer to Contestants ‘games of chance’ for money.It is the dominant element of skill or chance which determines the character of the game. The outcome or success in the Contests hosted on Gamezop platform is directly dependent on the Contestant’s effort, performance and skill. Contestant’s skill and knowledge of the Contest may be enhanced with practice. Additionally, Contests on the Platform have Rules and Contestants are encouraged to read, understand, and follow them.Different Contests will reward different skills, but each Contest will reward one or more skills, viz., knowledge of the game, familiarity with Rules, practice, hand-eye coordination, etc.The two main enactments dealing with gaming in India, are the pre-Independence Public Gambling Act, 1867 (“PGA”) and the Prize Competitions Act, 1955 (“PCA”) and both create a specific exception for Games of Skill.With respect to the PCA’s regulatory ambit, in the case of R. M. D. Chamarbaugwalla v. Union of India, the Supreme Court held that the only competitions that are sought to be controlled and regulated by the PCA are those in which success does not depend to any substantial degree of skill. Therefore, it ruled that games that are predominantly based on skill are excluded from the regulatory purview and penalising terms of the PCA. This position was reiterated in the case of News Television India Ltd. v. Ashok D. Waghmare where the Bombay High Court found the programme ‘Kaun Banega Crorepati’ to be based around a game of skill and not in violation of the PCA as it essentially required a participant to utilise his / her general knowledge and memory to provide answers to questions on various topics such as history, geography, sport, music, politics, cinema, science and technology, etc.
