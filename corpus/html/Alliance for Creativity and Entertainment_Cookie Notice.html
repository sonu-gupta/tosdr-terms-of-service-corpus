This notice explains what cookies are and the types of cookies the Alliance for Creativity and Entertainment  (“ACE”) uses on www.alliance4creativity.com (the “Site”).

What are cookies?

A “cookie” is a text file that websites send to a visitor’s computer or other Internet-connected devices to uniquely identify the visitor’s browser or to store information or settings in the browser.

Are cookies used on the Site and if so, why?

Yes. Cookies (including HTTP, HTML5 and Flash cookies) and similar technologies (such as web beacons and JavaScript) . are used to enhance the Site and provide services and functionality to users.

What types of cookies are used on the Site?

Strictly Necessary Cookies These cookies are necessary for the Site to work and cannot be disabled. . They enable navigation among different pages on the Site and allow you to use services and features on the Site. . Strictly necessary cookies are used to collect session ID and authentication data. . They are “session” cookies that expire when you terminate your browser session. Analytic Cookies

 

Analytic cookies allow us to collect data relating to your use of the Site to improve the performance and design of the Site and our services. . For these purposes, analytic cookies collect information about your device type, operating system type, browser type, domain, other system settings, IP-address, referring URLs, information on actions taken on the Site and the dates and times of your visits, as well as the language your system uses and the country and time zone in which your device is located. Analytic cookies include “persistent” cookies that expire after 30 years.

These cookies are provided by our third-party analytics provider, Piwik.

The information obtained through these third-party cookies will be disclosed to or collected directly by this third-party service provider. For more information about Piwik’s cookie use, click here.

 

How do I disable or remove cookies?

The settings to restrict the cookies on this Site can be found by visiting the “See Cookie Details” link in the Cookie banner upon first visiting the Site. Once the initial cookie settings have been made using the banner, they can be modified by visiting the “Cookie Settings” tab at the bottom of any webpage on this website.

You may also restrict or disable the use of cookies through your web browser. Each type of web browser offers ways to restrict and delete cookies. For more information on how to manage cookies, please visit the appropriate link below.
