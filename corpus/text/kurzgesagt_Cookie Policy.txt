What are cookies and how does kurzgesagt use them?
Cookies are very small text files that are automatically created by the browser and stored on your end device (computer, laptop, tablet, smartphone or similar) when you visit the website.
Cookies do not cause any damage to your end device, do not contain viruses, Trojans or other malware.
Cookies in this sense are small files or other technologies with which we can learn how you use our website.
This enables us, among other things, to offer you our website, to improve it and to display advertising on the site that is specifically tailored to your interests.
Cookies are very commonly used to make websites and services work and function at higher performance levels and to provide information about our user experience while using or interacting with our websites, products, services and advertisements.
However, this does not mean that we obtain immediate knowledge of your identity.
Some cookies only persist for the duration of your web session and expire when you leave the browser.
other cookies may persist longer than your web session, including after you leave the browser, for example by remembering you when you return to our website.
In the following, the most common types of cookies are explained for your understanding:


Session cookies: While you are active on a website, a session cookie is temporarily stored in the memory of your end device, in which a session identifier is stored, for example to prevent you having to log in again each time you change pages.
Session cookies are deleted when you log off or lose their validity as soon as your session has automatically expired.



Persistent cookies: Persistent cookies store a file on your mobile device for the period of time specified in the expiry date (storage period).
These cookies enable the website to remember your information and settings on your next visit.
This leads to faster and more convenient access, as you do not have to change your language settings for our portal again, for example.
When the storage period expires (see tables below), the cookie is automatically deleted on your terminal device.



Third-party cookies: Third-party cookies are session cookies or persistent cookies that originate from a provider other than the website operator.
For example, they may be used to collect information for advertising, custom content and web statistics.
