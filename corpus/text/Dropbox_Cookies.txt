What are cookies?
A cookie is small data file sent to your browser when you visit a website.
A pixel tag ("pixel") is a small piece of code belonging to a third-party that can be embedded on websites and in emails.
We use both our own cookies as well as third-party cookies.
See the âHow does Dropbox use cookiesâ section below for further information on third-party cookies we use.
We also use local storage, including HTML5 local storage and browser cache.
Local storage allows us to store information locally on your browser or device to improve your experience, such as to remember your settings for whether you prefer to view your folders in a list or as thumbnails.

Dropbox only retains personal data for as long as is needed for the purposes for which it was collected.
